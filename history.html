A brief-history

Discover the story behind web-world in a brief timeline from the key-figures to the technologies that shaped the foundations of this world.

Timeline

1946

Key Figure

Murray Leinster a.k.a "Adapter"

Author of Science Fiction

Technological advancements

Author of the short story titled "A logic Named Joe". While computing was still in it's infancy the story introduced the idea of a world connected by a personal computer network as well as its potential drawbacks.

Timeline

1991

Key Figure

Tim Berners-Lee

President/Founder/engineer of Web world

Technological advancements

On June 8th, 1991 the World Wide Web was announced to the world. On August 8th of the same year the world's 1st Web Page was launched at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in Switzerland. The web page which would become the foundation of what is now web world was hosted on NeXTcube, the world's first web server.

1996

Key Figure

Håkon Wium Lie

Engineer and computer scientist

Technological advancements

He is best known for proposing the concept of Cascading Style Sheets (CSS) while working with Tim Berners-Lee and Robert Cailliau at CERN in 1994. He is the reason behind the colours, fonts and style of all web webpages.
